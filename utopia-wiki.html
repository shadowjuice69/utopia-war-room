War Doctrine Updates

Each race provides a unique War Doctrine bonus when your kingdom is at war. The strength of the bonus depends on how many provinces of that race are in your kingdom.

Province CountDoctrine Bonus
1st Province+2.0%
Each additional Province+1%
Maximum Cap12.5%

Exception: Elf, Faery, and Halfling War Doctrines gain +2% strength from each additional province after the first, instead of +1% — reaching the 12.5% cap faster than other races.

This rewards early commitment to a race while still allowing deeper specialisation, up to a maximum total bonus of 12.5%.

Attacking and Defending

In War, successful Learn and Plunder attacks also capture land equal to 20% of the acres a Traditional March would have gained.

  • Massacre Peasant Damage in War: +5% (Additive)
  • Thief and Wizard damage unchanged.

Relations, Hostile Meter & FCF

Out of War Attack Penalty: Gain penalty lifted from 10% to 15%.

Hostile Meter Update: Changes to meter decay revert back to before Age 111.

  • Hostile Meter points decay on the 1st of each month by either 20% or 3 points, whichever is greater
  • Hostile Meter no longer resets for Ceasefires or Entering Wars

FCF becomes available for a defender when all of the following are met:

  • The defender is 30 points (in-range) or 15 points (out-of-range) below the aggressor on the hostility meter
  • The defender is not Hostile towards the aggressive kingdom
  • No attacks have been made against the aggressor for 3 ticks
  • The kingdom is lower in both Land and Networth than the aggressor

Penalty if within War Range (15% NW or Land): −2% Honor and −2% Science. No penalty if out of War Range.

Duration: 96 hours if out of range, 72 hours if in range, before it can be cancelled by either kingdom.

Each race contributes a War Doctrine that applies kingdom-wide during War. The first province of a race provides a stronger initial bonus, with each additional province contributing progressively less.

Province CountDoctrine Bonus

  • −1 Stealth Recovery
  • −15% Guilds Effectiveness
  • −15% Thieves' Dens Effectiveness

Relations — DFCF Reintroduced

Defensive Force Ceasefire (DFCF) becomes available when ALL of the following are met:

  • Kingdom is 45 pts (in-range) or 30 pts (out-of-range) below aggressor on hostility meter
  • Aggressor has NOT been given Hostile status
  • No attacks have been made against aggressor for 3 ticks
  • Kingdom is lower in both Land and Networth than aggressor

Penalty if within War Range: −1.75% Honor and −1.75% Science. No penalty if out of War Range.

Duration: 48 hours before it can be cancelled by either kingdom.

Hostile Meter Decay: Now decays after 48 hours of going hostile; ramp rate scales with meter points.

Attacking & Defending

Military Efficiency

Base Military Efficiency

Base ME = (33 + 67 × (Effective Wage Rate / 100)^0.25) × Ruby Dragon × Multi-Attack Protection Bonus

Wage range: 20% (77.8% ME) to 200% (112.7% ME)
Effective wage rate changes slowly — takes ~96hrs to go from 20% to 200%

Offensive Military Efficiency

OME = (Base ME + Training Grounds Bonus + Honor Bonus) × Science × Race × Personality × Fanaticism × Bloodlust × Ritual

Defensive Military Efficiency

DME = (Base ME + Forts Bonus + Honor Bonus) × Science × Race × Personality × Minor Protection × Greater Protection × Fanaticism × Plague × Ritual

Attack Times

Attack Time = Base Attack Time × Race × Personality × Barracks Bonus × Quick Feet × Attack Type × War × NW Mod × Ritual Bonus

Raw & Modified Offense

Raw Off = (Soldiers × Soldier Off + Aggression) + (Off Specs × Off value) + (Elites × Elite Off) + (Horses × Horse Off) + (Mercs × 8) + (Prisoners × 8)

Mod Off = Raw Off × (OME + General Bonus)

Minimum Offense to Win

Send Mod Offense at least 1 greater than defender's Mod Defense to guarantee a win.

Conquest only requires 51% of opponent's Defense.

Mod Defense = MAX(Raw Defense × DME, Land) — all land is protected by minimum 1 defense per acre.

Attack Gains Formula

Gains = Target Resource × Attack Type × RPNW × RKNW × Multi-Attack Protection × Race × Personality × Castles × Relations × Stance × Siege Science × Emerald Dragon × Attack Time Adj × Ritual × Anonymity × Mist

Province NW Factor (RPNW)

rpnw = Target NW / Self NW

rpnw < 0.567 → Gains = 0
0.567 < rpnw < 0.9 → 3 × rpnw − 1.7
0.9 < rpnw < 1.1 → 1 (full gains)
1.1 < rpnw < 1.6 → −2 × rpnw + 3.2
rpnw > 1.6 → Gains = 0

Kingdom NW Factor (RKNW)

rknw = Target KD avg NW / Self KD avg NW

rknw < 0.5 → 0.8
0.5 < rknw < 0.9 → rknw/2 + 0.55
rknw > 0.9 → 1

Dragons

Dragons are sent by the Monarch/Steward and wreak havoc for 48 ticks or until slain. Celestite Dragon removed in Age 115.

Dragon Behavior

  • Troops slay dragons at their offensive strength (soldiers = 3 damage each, elites = their off value)
  • Spells like Bloodlust/Aggression do NOT increase dragon slay damage
  • Valor science increases dragon slaying strength
  • Thieves, Wizards, and Prisoners cannot slay dragons
  • Send range: 20% smaller to 25% larger in NW — removed during War
  • Only Monarch/Steward can send; all provinces can fund
  • Dragon auto-cancelled on EoWCF or Ceasefire

Kingdom Rituals

Kingdom Rituals provide kingdom-wide bonuses. Started by Monarch/Steward. Only one active at a time, though a new one can be started before the current one ends.

Completing a Ritual

Must successfully cast the ritual spell a minimum of 3× the number of provinces (min 15 provinces for this calculation) within 48 hours of starting.

Ritual can be overcasted for increased effects:

Ritual Effectiveness = 1 + (2 × SQRT(X − MAX(45, KD_provs × 3))) / 100
where X = number of ritual casts (when X ≥ 3× provinces)

Ritual strength diminishes by 0.25% per tick.
Duration: 48–120 hours. Rune cost multiplier: 6×. Mana cost: 2% per cast.

Ambush Formula

Raw Offense Required = [(Elites sent × Racial Elite Defense Value) + (Off Specs sent × Racial Def Spec Value) + (Soldiers sent × Racial Soldier OFFENSE Value)] × 0.80

⚠️ Soldiers defend at their Racial Soldier OFFENSE value (currently 3) — NOT their defense value of 0
⚠️ Off Specs defend at their RACE'S Def Spec value (not their offense value)
⚠️ Elites defend at their RACE'S Elite Defense value
⚠️ Horses do NOT provide defense in an ambush
⚠️ War Horses do NOT boost your offense in an ambush
⚠️ Sending more than 1 General does NOT increase ambush offense
⚠️ Province using Anonymity or War Spoils CANNOT be ambushed
⚠️ An army can only be ambushed ONCE
⚠️ Aggression spell on YOUR province adds +2 to each of YOUR soldiers' offense when calculating your Raw Offense to ambush — but Aggression on the ENEMY army does NOT increase their ambush defense value (their soldiers still defend at base 3)

Recommended: oversend by at least 7% to account for SoM inaccuracies

Example: Enemy sent 5,000 Orc elites (2 def) + 500 off specs (10 def) + 100 soldiers (3 off = 3 def in ambush):

Ambush Defense = (5,000×2) + (500×10) + (100×3) = 10,000 + 5,000 + 300 = 15,300

Min Raw Offense needed = 15,300 × 0.80 = 12,240 raw offense points

With 7% oversend buffer = 13,097 raw offense points

Aid & Trade Balance

Trade Balance: Each province maintains a running record of aid sent and received
Sending aid → you gain Trade Surplus · recipient gains Trade Deficit

Tax Rate: Kicks in when recipient's negative Trade Balance exceeds 400% of their Networth
Tax confiscates a portion of the traded goods
If province is below KD average NW, the KD average NW is used instead of their own NW

Trade Balance Decay: 10% of Trade Balance forgiven per Utopian Month (does NOT decay during War)

Aid during War: Shipment efficiency higher (~80–95%), critical for chain recovery
Explosions spell: 50% chance to reduce aid shipment to 55–80% of original size
Sending Aid counts as an aggressive action (minimum 24hrs before accepting invitations)

Aid limits: Max single shipment = 400% of your own NW in resources (the "one max aid shipment" threshold before tax applies to recipient).

Aid exemption: Aid to needy provinces (below average NW) is sometimes exempt from taxation, but still accumulates in Trade Balance.

Building Effect Types (Detailed)

TypeDescriptionBE ImpactExamples
Capacity Buildings Simply create space — no efficiency issues NOT affected by BE Homes, Stables, Dungeons
Flat Rate Buildings Fixed amount per building regardless of % land Affected by BE (need workers) Towers (12 runes/tower), Farms (60 bushels/farm)
% Based Buildings Effect based on % of total land — diminishing returns Affected by BE + diminishing returns Banks, Guilds, Training Grounds, Barracks, Forts, Castles

Guilds — Stacking Probability (Flat Rate): Guilds produce wizards as a stacking chance per tick, NOT a guaranteed flat amount. Example: 835 Guilds = 16.7 wizards/tick = guaranteed 16 wizards + 70% chance of a 17th wizard that tick. This is NOT affected by Production Science or BE.

Homes — Capacity Building: Homes provide +10 population capacity (on top of the 25 from built land = 35 total per acre), +0.3 peasants/tick stacking chance (30% chance per Home acre), and increase birth rates. They employ NO peasants and do not count toward jobs.

Dungeons — Capacity Building: Hold 25 prisoners per dungeon acre. Deserting troops fill dungeons first before being lost. Capacity unaffected by BE.

Stables — Capacity Building: Capacity unaffected by BE. War Horses cannot exceed 1 per military unit sent.
